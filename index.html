PHYSICS Chapter: Work and Energy

Questions and Answers

Short Answer Type Questions

1) When is work said to be done?
Work is said to be done when force is applied on a body and it is displaced.
2) Condition for work to be done?
Force must be applied on the body, and the body must tend to move.
3) When is no work done?
i) When the force applied on a body does not move it.
ii) When the angle between force and displacement is 90°.
4) Identify if work is done or not in given cases.
a) No work
b) Work is done
c) No work
d) Work is done
5) Write the formula for work.
Work = Force × Displacement (W = F × D).
6) What is the SI unit of work?
SI unit of work is Joule (J), where 1 J = 1 Newton × 1 meter.
7) Factors on which work depends?
i) Force applied on body
ii) Distance moved by the body.
8) Define energy.
Energy is defined as the ability to do work.
9) SI unit of energy?
The SI Unit of Energy is Joule (J).
10) When does a body possess one joule of energy?
If it can do one Joule of work, it has one Joule of energy.
11) Relation between work and energy?
Work is directly related to energy. To do more work, more energy is required.
12) Name two types of mechanical energy.
i) Potential Energy
ii) Kinetic Energy
13) Define Potential Energy (PE).
The energy possessed by a body due to its state of rest or position is called Potential Energy.
14) Give two examples of Potential Energy.
a) A hanging fan
b) A stretched catapult
15) Which bucket has greater potential energy: one on ground or one kept on second floor?
The bucket on the second floor has greater Potential Energy.
16) Write the formula for Potential Energy.
PE = m × g × h
17) Define Kinetic Energy (KE).
The energy possessed by a body due to its motion is called Kinetic Energy.
18) Factors affecting Kinetic Energy
i) Mass of the body
ii) Speed (velocity) of the body
19) Which toy car has more KE, car A or car B?
Toy car B will have greater kinetic energy.
20) If velocity increases, what happens to KE?
Kinetic Energy increases.
21) Formula for KE
KE = ½ × m × v²
22) KE can also be written as?
½ m v²
23) Which energy is stored energy?
Potential Energy
24) Identify energy type in given cases
a) KE
b) PE
c) PE
d) KE
e) KE
f) KE
g) PE
25) Give examples of transformation of energy
i) Electric → Light
ii) Electric → Heat
iii) Electric → Sound
iv) Sound → Electric
v) Electric → Mechanical
vi) Mechanical → Electric

Long Answer Type Questions

1) Factors on which gravitational potential energy depends?
It depends on mass of the body and gravitational force (g).
2) Which body has greater PE, A or B?
Body B has greater Potential Energy.
3) Derive expression for Potential Energy.
a) Force needed to lift a body = Weight = m × g N
b) Work done = F × d = m × g × h
c) Therefore, Potential Energy (PE) = m × g × h J
4) Can a body at rest have potential energy?
Yes, for example a ball kept on the 10th floor has potential energy due to its height.
5) Explain PE to KE conversion with example.
A snowball kept at a hilltop has potential energy. When released, it rolls down as PE converts into KE.
6) Name energy conversion in free fall.
Potential Energy converts into Kinetic Energy.
