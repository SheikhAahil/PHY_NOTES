PHYSICS Chapter: Work and Energy

Questions and Answers

Short Answer Type Questions

1) When is work said to be done?
Work is said to be done when a force is applied on a body and the body is displaced.
6) What is the SI unit of work?
The SI unit of work is Joule (J), where 1 Joule = 1 Newton × 1 meter.
8) Define energy.
Energy is defined as the ability to do work.
16) Write the formula for Potential Energy.
Potential Energy (PE) = m × g × h
21) Write the formula for Kinetic Energy.
Kinetic Energy (KE) = ½ × m × v²

Long Answer Type Questions

3) Derive the expression for potential energy.
Solution:
a) Force needed to lift a body = Weight = m × g N
b) Work done = F × d = m × g × h
c) Therefore, Potential Energy = m × g × h (J).
5) Explain conversion of Potential Energy into Kinetic Energy.
A snowball kept on a hill has potential energy due to its position. When released, this energy converts into kinetic energy as it rolls down.
