Questions and Answers

Short Answer Type Questions

1) Define work
Work is said to be done when force is applied on a body and it is displaced.
2) When does a force perform work?
When it is applied on a body, and the body tends to move
3) State two conditions when no work is done?
i) When the force applied on a body does not move it.
ii) When the angle between force and displacement is 90°.
4) In which cases work is being done
a) No work
b) Work is done
c) No work
d) Work is done
5) Write the expression for work.
Work = Force × Displacement (W = F × D).
6) What is the SI unit of work?Define it
SI unit of work is Joule (J), where 1 J = 1 Newton × 1 meter.
7)State two factors on which work depends?
i) Force applied on body
ii) Distance moved by the body.
8) Define energy.
Energy is defined as the ability to do work.
9) SI unit of energy?
The SI Unit of Energy is Joule (J).
10) Define one joule of energy
If it can do one Joule of work, it has one Joule of energy.
11) Relation between work and energy?
Work is directly related to energy. To do more work, more energy is required.
12) Name two types of mechanical energy.
i) Potential Energy
ii) Kinetic Energy
13) Define Potential Energy (PE).State its SI unit
The energy possessed by a body due to its state of rest or position is called Potential Energy.Its SI unit is joule(J)
14) Give two examples of Potential Energy.
a) A hanging fan
b) A stretched catapult
15) Which bucket has greater potential energy: one on ground or one kept on second floor?
The bucket on the second floor has greater Potential Energy.
16) Write the expression for Potential Energy.
PE = m × g × h
17) Define Kinetic Energy (KE).Give one example
The energy possessed by a body due to its motion is called Kinetic Energy.
Eg-F1 car during race
18)State two factors affecting Kinetic Energy
i) Mass of the body
ii) Speed (velocity) of the body
19) Which toy car has more KE, car A or car B?When A has mass of 200g and B has mass of 500g
Toy car B will have greater kinetic energy.
20) A cyclist doubles his speed. How will his kinetic energy change: Increase or re
Kinetic Energy increases.
21) Expression for KE, explaining the meanings of symbol used
KE = ½ × m × v². Where m is mass of the body and v is speed(velocity) of the body

Body B has greater Potential Energy.

b) Work done = F × d = m × g × h
c) Therefore, Potential Energy (PE) = m × g × h J

5) Give an example to show the conversion of potential energy to kinetic energy when put in use
A snowball kept at a hilltop has potential energy PE. When released, it rolls down. In the half way down the ball has 1/2PE and 1/2KE and on reaching the plain PE converts into KE .
